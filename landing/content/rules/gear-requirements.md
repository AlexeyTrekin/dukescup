## Sabre

The tournament is dedicated to late-19 century fencing, and the weapons should correspond to the historical examples. Ð¢he sabre should have a bell guard or multi-branch guard and moderately curved or straight blade. Other configurations may not be allowed, in case of doubts contact us.

- Steel blade
- Full length up to 100 cm (in a straight line)
- Weight: 750-1000 gr
- Point of balance: 7-12 cm from the guard
- Reasonably flexible blade, without residual bend 
- Recommended loop point; other types of point blunt should be additionally protected with duct tape, rubber or leather overlay
- The blade and guard should not have nicks, notches, barbs or other elements dangerous to the opponent or the gear.

## Sword & buckler

**Buckler** - round, up to 35 cm in diameter. Material: wood/plastic.

**Sword:** arming sword or sidesword; straight blade, simple crossguard or branched guard (no bell/ cup hilts).

- Length up to 110 cm for sideswords; up to 100 cm for arming swords.
- Weight 900-1200 grams

## Mandatory protective equipment

Every fighter must use the following equipment

- Fencing mask, certified at Level 1 (350N) at least
- Mask overlay protecting the top of the mask and the back of the head and neck
- Protective jacket without gaps
- Rigid breast protection under the jacket
- Rigid groin protection (for men)
- Protection for throat and neck (a separate gorget, or a similar protection built in the jacket), blade catcher is highly recommended
- Rigid elbow and forearm protection
- Rigid knee and shin protection
- Fencing gloves for both hands, suitable for the weapon

**NB! Open metal elements of protection, except for the mask, are forbidden!**
